## A Conversation We've Been Growing Towards

Over the last few years, our conversations have repeatedly returned to similar questions from different starting points.

Resilience has been cultivating the conditions for communities, organisations and territories to become more capable of stewarding complex living systems. Through participatory governance, regenerative design, systems thinking and ecosystem stewardship, you have developed deep experience in creating environments where collective intelligence can emerge and communities can organise around shared purpose.

At Prisma, our journey has taken us toward complementary questions.

Rather than beginning with places and territories, we have been exploring the infrastructure that enables people, organisations and networks to learn together over time. Through Action Learning Journeys, publishing, participatory evaluation, distributed coordination, contribution accounting and organisational infrastructure, we have been asking how collective learning can become persistent, visible and continuously generative.

Although our work has evolved in different contexts, we increasingly recognise that both organisations are responding to the same underlying challenge:

How do we increase the adaptive capacity of people, organisations and places so they can navigate complexity together?

We believe this shared direction is worth exploring deliberately.

## Parallel Journeys, Shared Questions

**Both organisations recognise that today's challenges cannot be addressed through isolated expertise alone.**

They require new ways of organising.

New ways of learning.

Rather than treating projects as isolated interventions, both Resilience and Prisma have been developing approaches that cultivate long-term capability.

Some of the questions we both appear to be exploring include:

* How can communities become increasingly capable of stewarding their own places?
* How can organisational learning become more continuous?
* How can locally-relevant knowledge be shared safely?
* How can governance become participatory without becoming overwhelming?
* How can we enabled distributed coordination?
* How can regeneration become something that is continually practiced, reflected upon and evolved?

These are not questions either organisation owns. They are questions we are both participating in.

## Where Our Works Meet

Resilience has developed exceptional capability in designing and facilitating regenerative processes.

Prisma has been developing infrastructure that enables those kinds of processes to remain visible, learnable and connected over time.

Action Learning Journeys emerged within Prisma as a way of holding action, reflection, publication and ongoing coordination as one continuous process rather than separate activities. Technology is secondary; just part of enabling ways of organising and learning. 

- Publishing is an ongoing process of articulating and sharing the systems being worked on.
- Evaluation is an ongoing process of seeing value.
- Contribution accounting helps attribute and resource individuals and collectives.
- Infrastructure supports these processes to grow from standalone experiments into part of an organisation's practices. 
- The purpose is organisational capacity and increasing collective learning.

## Why This Partnership is a Natural Fit

Over the last decade, Resilience has cultivated an extraordinary ecosystem of relationships, methodologies and regional partnerships. The communities you work with are active participants in governing and stewarding complex living systems.

Prisma has been working toward complementary capabilities that support these kinds of processes over long-term time horizons.

Where Resilience specialises in cultivating emergence within communities and places, Prisma focuses on complementing that emergence with sovereign digital capabilities, that those same communities and places own.

Together, these represent different layers of the same purpose. One develops regeneration in place. The other develops digital infrastructure from a regenerative design orientation. Both are regenerative practice. 

Our work aims to strengthen yours, by design.
