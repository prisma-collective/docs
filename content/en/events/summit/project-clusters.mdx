# Project Clusters

Text fields from 107 team registrations were consolidated and converted into semantic embeddings. These embeddings were clustered to identify natural groupings of projects based on similarity, and clusters were labeled to represent distinct themes. Each project was then assigned weighted membership across these clusters, allowing projects to reflect multiple relevant domains rather than a single label. The resulting dataset enables exploration and visualization of how projects relate across different thematic dimensions and perspectives.

The team registration fields from which these embeddings were generated were:

1. Please define your project's problem statement
2. Please describe the role your project plays in the region
3. Please describe how you see the potential for change in your context
4. Please describe how your perception of your local context and its potential has changed over the course of the programme
5. Please describe how you are organising yourselves to work on that potential

This particular analysis was conducted on February 3rd, 2026, using `N=15` as the number of clusters to aim for. Variations in parameters may yield different results. Problem statements are presented alongside correlation scores for further interpretation.
