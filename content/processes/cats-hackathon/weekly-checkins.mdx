# Weekly Check-in Calls

Throughout the CATS Hackathon (November 1, 2024 - January 6, 2025), Next Trend Hub hosts **bi-weekly check-in calls** to track progress, answer questions, and provide guidance to all participants and teams.

## Schedule

### Regular Meeting Times

**📅 Wednesdays**  
**Time:** 6:00 PM WAT (West Africa Time)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Focus:** Progress updates, Q&A, troubleshooting

**📅 Saturdays**  
**Time:** 1:00 PM WAT (West Africa Time)  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Focus:** Milestone reviews, planning ahead, community building

### Meeting Platform

**Platform:** [To be announced - likely Zoom or Google Meet]  
**Link:** Shared in Telegram group before each call  
**Recording:** Calls are recorded and shared post-meeting

## Call Format

### Typical Agenda (45-60 mins)

1. **Welcome & Attendance** (5 mins)
   - Roll call / check-in
   - Agenda overview

2. **Previous Week Recap** (5 mins)
   - What was supposed to happen
   - What actually happened
   - [View meeting minutes →](/processes/cats-hackathon/meeting-minutes)

3. **Team/Participant Updates** (20-25 mins)
   - Teams share progress (2-3 mins each)
   - Challenges encountered
   - Wins and breakthroughs

4. **Guidance & Teaching** (10-15 mins)
   - Mini-lessons on relevant topics
   - Methodology deep-dives
   - Technical tips

5. **Q&A Session** (10-15 mins)
   - Open floor for questions
   - Problem-solving together
   - Resource sharing

6. **Next Steps & Assignments** (5 mins)
   - What to accomplish before next call
   - Upcoming deadlines
   - Reminders

## What to Prepare

### Before Each Check-in Call

**Individual Participants (Phases 1-3):**
- ✅ Summary of community research conducted
- ✅ Key findings or observations
- ✅ Questions or blockers
- ✅ Progress on assigned tasks

**Teams (Phases 4-8):**
- ✅ What we accomplished this week
- ✅ What we're working on now
- ✅ Challenges we need help with
- ✅ Questions for mentors or organizers

**Everyone:**
- ✅ Review previous [meeting minutes](/processes/cats-hackathon/meeting-minutes)
- ✅ Bring specific questions (not just "we're stuck")
- ✅ Be ready to share screen if demonstrating work

## Reporting Format

### Phase 1-2 (Individual Updates)

When sharing your update, cover:
1. **Community Research:** Where did you go? Who did you talk to?
2. **Findings:** What problems did you discover?
3. **Evidence:** What voice notes or documentation do you have?
4. **Questions:** What do you need clarity on?

### Phase 3-4 (Team Formation Updates)

When sharing your update, cover:
1. **Team Status:** Team name, members, roles
2. **Problem Focus:** Which problem are you pursuing?
3. **Research Progress:** Additional validation done?
4. **Concept Status:** Early solution ideas?

### Phase 5-7 (Build Updates)

When sharing your update, cover:
1. **Progress:** What features/components were built?
2. **Technical Stack:** Any changes or decisions?
3. **Blockers:** What's slowing you down?
4. **Next Steps:** What will you work on next?
5. **Demo:** (If applicable) Show what you've built

## Attendance Policy

### Expected Participation

- **Strongly recommended:** Attend every check-in call
- **Minimum requirement:** Attend at least 1 call per week (Wednesday OR Saturday)
- **Critical calls:** Phase milestone check-ins (noted in advance)

### If You Must Miss a Call

1. **Notify in advance** - Post in Telegram group
2. **Review the recording** - Catch up on what you missed
3. **Read the meeting minutes** - Posted after each call
4. **Reach out with questions** - Don't let gaps accumulate

## Special Check-in Calls

### Milestone Check-ins

Certain check-ins mark major milestones:

**Nov 6/9:** First check-in - Program orientation complete  
**Nov 20/23:** Pre-Idea-Shaping - Prep for first in-person session  
**Dec 4/7:** Pre-Prototyping - Prep for second in-person session  
**Dec 10:** Pre-submission - First prototype due  
**Dec 12:** Final prep - Last call before Final Hack Day

These calls are **especially important** to attend.

## Communication Channels

### Between Check-ins

**For urgent questions:**
- Telegram group chat (fastest response)
- WhatsApp community
- Email to Next Trend Hub organizers

**For non-urgent updates:**
- Post in Telegram group
- Wait for next check-in call
- Document in your team's process page

### Emergency Support

If your team faces a critical blocker:
- **Don't wait for the next call**
- Tag @organizers in Telegram
- Request ad-hoc meeting if needed
- Next Trend Hub will respond within 24 hours

## Meeting Etiquette

### Do's ✅

- Join on time
- Mute when not speaking
- Turn on video if possible (builds community)
- Share specific, actionable questions
- Take notes during the call
- Respect others' speaking time

### Don'ts ❌

- Join late without notification
- Dominate conversation time
- Ask questions already answered in docs
- Multitask obviously (coding, gaming, etc.)
- Talk over others
- Leave early without notice
