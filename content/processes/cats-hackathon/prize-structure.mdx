# Prize Structure

The CATS Hackathon offers prizes at **two levels**: local prizes distributed by Next Trend Hub (Lagos), and continental prizes for teams selected from across participating African countries.

## Local Prize Pool: 1,000 ADA

Each hub participating in the CATS Hackathon receives a **fixed allocation of 1,000 ADA** to distribute among top-performing teams.

### Distribution Based on Participation

The number of prizes awarded depends on the size of the hub's participation:

#### For Hubs with < 150 Participants

**Criteria:** Fewer than 150 total participants (or fewer than 15 teams of 5 members each)

**Prize Distribution (Top 3):**

| Position | Prize Amount | Percentage |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| 🥇 **1st Place** | 500 ADA | 50% |
| 🥈 **2nd Place** | 300 ADA | 30% |
| 🥉 **3rd Place** | 200 ADA | 20% |

**Total:** 1,000 ADA distributed among 3 teams

---

#### For Hubs with ≥ 150 Participants

**Criteria:** 150 or more total participants (or 15+ teams of 5 members each)

**Prize Distribution (Top 4):**

| Position | Prize Amount | Percentage |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| 🥇 **1st Place** | 500 ADA | 50% |
| 🥈 **2nd Place** | 300 ADA | 30% |
| 🥉 **3rd Place** | 200 ADA | 20% |
| 🏅 **4th Place** | 200 ADA | Split from 3rd |

**Total:** 1,000 ADA distributed among 4 teams

**Note:** When a hub qualifies for 4 prizes, the 3rd and 4th place teams share what would have been the 3rd place allocation (200 ADA each).

## Next Trend Hub Lagos

**Current Status:** 13 teams × 5 members = **65 participants**

**Prize Structure:** Top 3 teams (500/300/200 ADA)

**Eligibility:** All 13 participating teams are eligible for local prizes based on their performance during local evaluation (January 6, 2025).

## Continental Grand Prize

Teams that win at the local level advance to **continental evaluation** for the grand prize pool.

### Selection Process

1. **Local Winners Selected** (January 6, 2025)
   - Each participating hub selects their top 3-4 teams
   - Winners receive local ADA prizes
   - Projects forwarded to CATS/WADA for continental review

2. **Continental Pool Created**
   - All local winners from participating African countries enter the pool
   - Projects reviewed by CATS/WADA evaluation committee
   - Teams judged against continental standards

3. **Grand Prize Winners Announced** (January 9, 2025)
   - **Top 5 teams** selected from the entire continental pool
   - Winners receive additional prizes

### Grand Prize Awards

**For the Top 5 Continental Winners:**

🏆 **Additional ADA Allocation**
- Amount TBA (to be announced by CATS/WADA)
- Distributed among the top 5 teams
- In addition to any local prizes already won

✈️ **Sponsored Trip to Kenya**
- All-expenses-paid trip for team representatives
- Attend CATS/WADA final event and awards ceremony
- Networking with other African innovators
- Potential investor/partner connections

The CATS Hackathon spans **multiple African countries**, each with local hubs running concurrent programs. Next Trend Hub (Lagos, Nigeria) competes alongside hubs from:

- Kenya

**This means:** Winning locally is an achievement, but advancing to the Top 5 continental requires excellence among hundreds of teams across Africa.

## What Teams Compete For

### Minimum Achievement: Participation

Every team that completes the program receives:
- ✅ Certificate of participation
- ✅ Documentation of their action-learning journey
- ✅ Portfolio project demonstrating community research skills
- ✅ Connections with mentors and peers

### Local Recognition: Top 3-4

Teams selected as local winners (Next Trend Hub) receive:
- 💰 ADA prizes (500/300/200 ADA)
- 🏆 Recognition as Lagos regional winners
- 📄 Advancement to continental evaluation
- 🎯 Potential for grand prize selection

### Continental Excellence: Top 5

Teams selected for the continental grand prize receive:
- 💎 Additional ADA allocation
- ✈️ Trip to Kenya for final ceremony
- 🌍 Recognition across African tech ecosystem
- 🤝 Investor and partner introductions
- 📰 Media coverage and visibility

## Evaluation Criteria

Prizes are awarded based on:

1. **Action-Learning Journey Quality**
   - Depth of community research
   - Quality of evidence gathered (voice notes, interviews)
   - Rigor of insight and hypothesis formulation

2. **Solution Innovation & Feasibility**
   - Technical implementation quality
   - Cardano blockchain integration (where applicable)
   - Scalability and sustainability

3. **Community Impact Potential**
   - Number of people who could benefit
   - Significance of problem solved
   - Evidence of community validation

4. **"Build What Matters" Alignment**
   - Authenticity of problem discovery
   - Community-driven approach
   - Regenerative principles demonstrated

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can a team win both local and continental prizes?**  
A: Yes! Local prizes are guaranteed to local winners. If you're then selected for Top 5 continental, you receive additional prizes on top of local winnings.

**Q: How many team members can go to Kenya if we win?**  
A: Details TBA by CATS/WADA. Typically 1-2 representatives per team are sponsored.

**Q: What if our hub grows to 150+ participants?**  
A: Prize structure adjusts automatically. Current count (65) means Top 3 structure applies.

**Q: Are prizes distributed in ADA or converted to fiat?**  
A: Distributed in ADA (Cardano cryptocurrency). Teams receive wallet instructions.

**Q: What happens to teams that don't win prizes?**  
A: All teams gain valuable experience, documentation, portfolio projects, and connections. Many non-winners go on to develop their projects further post-hackathon.

**Q: Can I still participate if I'm not competitive about prizes?**  
A: Absolutely! Many participants join for learning, networking, and community impact rather than prizes.
