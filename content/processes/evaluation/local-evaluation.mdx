# Local Evaluation Process

**Date:** January 6, 2025  
**Location:** Next Trend Hub, Lagos  
**Evaluating:** All 13 teams (65 participants)

## Overview

The local evaluation determines which 3-4 teams from Next Trend Hub will:
- Receive local ADA prizes (500/300/200 ADA)
- Advance to continental evaluation
- Compete for Top 5 global prizes

## Judging Panel

### Panel Composition
- **Next Trend Hub Leadership** (2-3 judges)
- **Local Mentors** (2-3 judges)
- **Community Representatives** (1-2 judges)
- **Technical Expert** (1 judge, optional)

**Total:** 5-8 judges

### Judge Roles
- Review all team documentation pages
- Watch December 13 presentations (recorded or live)
- Score teams using evaluation criteria
- Deliberate to select winners
- Provide feedback to teams

## Evaluation Timeline

| Date | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Dec 13 | Teams present, submit documentation |
| Dec 14-31 | Teams can refine documentation |
| Jan 1-5 | Judges review submissions independently |
| Jan 6 | Panel meets, deliberates, announces winners |

## Scoring Process

### Step 1: Independent Scoring (Jan 1-5)

Each judge reviews all 13 teams independently:

**What Judges Review:**
1. All 7 team documentation pages
2. Voice notes (sample 3-5 per team)
3. Prototype/solution (if accessible)
4. Presentation deck or video (from Dec 13)

**Scoring Sheet:** Each judge scores teams on 6 criteria (100 points total)

| Criterion | Weight | Max Points |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Ground Truth Quality | 25% | 25 |
| Insight Formulation | 15% | 15 |
| Hypothesis Clarity | 10% | 10 |
| Opportunity Definition | 10% | 10 |
| Solution Quality | 25% | 25 |
| Impact Potential | 15% | 15 |

[Full criteria details →](/processes/evaluation)

### Step 2: Score Aggregation (Jan 6 morning)

- Average scores across all judges
- Identify top contenders
- Note any major score discrepancies (discuss in deliberation)

### Step 3: Panel Deliberation (Jan 6 afternoon)

**Deliberation Process:**
1. Review aggregated scores
2. Discuss top 5-6 teams
3. Address score discrepancies
4. Consider qualitative factors:
   - Quality of community research
   - Authenticity of journey
   - Team effort and collaboration
   - Potential for continued development

**Decision Criteria:**
- Top 3 teams clear (if less than 150 participants)
- Top 4 teams if 150+ participants (Next Trend Hub has 65, so Top 3)

### Step 4: Winner Announcement (Jan 6 evening)

- Announce via Next Trend Hub channels
- Notify teams individually
- Share feedback summaries
- Announce prize distribution

## What Judges Look For

### Ground Truth Quality (25 points)
✅ **Strong Evidence:**
- 10-15+ voice notes per team
- Diverse interviewee profiles
- Specific quotes and examples
- Clear documentation

❌ **Weak Evidence:**
- Few voice notes (less than 10)
- Homogenous sample
- Vague summaries
- Missing links

### Insight Formulation (15 points)
✅ **Strong Analysis:**
- Clear themes from data
- Root cause identification
- Pattern recognition
- Superior interests defined

❌ **Weak Analysis:**
- Superficial patterns
- No root cause analysis
- Jumping to solutions
- Ignoring contradictions

### Hypothesis Clarity (10 points)
✅ **Strong Hypothesis:**
- Specific target audience
- Measurable problem
- Root cause stated
- Testable prediction

❌ **Weak Hypothesis:**
- Vague audience
- Unmeasurable outcome
- No root cause
- Actually a solution statement

### Opportunity Definition (10 points)
✅ **Strong Exploration:**
- 3-5 options considered
- Evaluation criteria clear
- Scoring matrix used
- Trade-offs acknowledged

❌ **Weak Exploration:**
- Only one option
- No justification
- Ignoring feasibility
- Copying existing products

### Solution Quality (25 points)
✅ **Strong Solution:**
- Functional prototype
- Addresses validated problem
- Usable by target audience
- Evidence of testing

❌ **Weak Solution:**
- Broken or incomplete
- Doesn't match hypothesis
- Unusable interface
- No testing done

### Impact Potential (15 points)
✅ **Strong Impact:**
- Clear scale (how many people)
- Significant improvement
- Sustainability plan
- Community validation

❌ **Weak Impact:**
- Vague benefit claims
- Minimal improvement
- Not sustainable
- No validation

## Feedback Process

After winners announced:

**All Teams Receive:**
- Score summary (aggregate, not individual judge scores)
- Written feedback (2-3 paragraphs)
- Strengths identified
- Areas for improvement

**Winners Receive:**
- Detailed feedback for continental competition
- Suggestions for strengthening submission
- Connection to CATS/WADA resources

## Appeals Process

**No formal appeals**, but:
- Teams can request clarification on feedback
- Hub leadership available for questions
- Scores are final once announced

## Continental Advancement

**Top 3 teams automatically advance to:**
- Continental evaluation pool
- Compete with Top 3-4 from all other hubs
- Judged by CATS/WADA panel
- Eligible for Top 5 global prizes

## FAQ

**Q: Can non-winning teams continue working on solutions?**  
A: Absolutely! Hackathon is just the beginning.

**Q: Will we get detailed feedback?**  
A: Yes, all teams receive score summaries and written feedback.

**Q: When do winners get ADA prizes?**  
A: Distribution process announced after Jan 6 announcement.

**Q: Can we update documentation between Dec 13 and Jan 6?**  
A: Yes, deadline is Jan 6 for final updates.

**Q: What if there's a tie?**  
A: Panel deliberates based on qualitative assessment and overall journey quality.
