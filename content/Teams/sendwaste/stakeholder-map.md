## CARETAKERS
*Those Keeping Systems Running*

### Informal Waste Collectors
- **Ibrahim (15 years, Kubwa):** *"My problem is not finding waste. My problem is proving to buyers what I collected."*
- **Power:** High operational, Low institutional
- **Need:** Proof of work for bank credit

### Community Health Workers
- **Nurse Joy (Lugbe PHC):** Treats children repeatedly for waste-related illness
- **Power:** Medium influence, Low resources
- **Need:** Disease reduction data

### Environmental NGOs
- Green Environment Initiative, Nigeria Environmental Society
- **Power:** Medium reach, Low implementation
- **Need:** Verifiable impact data for funders

---

## STAKEHOLDERS
*Institutions and Formal Actors*

### Abuja Environmental Protection Board (AEPB)
- **Anonymous Official:** *"We budget â‚¦50 million annually but verify almost nothing."*
- **Power:** High authority, Low enforcement capacity
- **Need:** Real-time contractor accountability

### Federal Ministry of Environment
- Sets national climate policy and ESG targets
- **Power:** Very high policy, Low ground visibility
- **Need:** Verified carbon credit generation

### Private Waste Management Companies
- Licensed contractors struggling with reputation
- **Power:** Medium-high contracts, Eroding trust
- **Need:** Transparent service proof

### Corporate Sector (ESG Requirements)
- Banks, telecoms, oil companies with CSR budgets
- **Power:** High capital control
- **Need:** Verifiable ESG reporting data
- **Pipeline:** Regional bank (â‚¦1.2M contract potential)

### Estate Management Associations
- Gwarimpa, Maitama resident groups
- **Power:** Medium aggregation leverage
- **Need:** Reliable contractor verification

## EMERGING LEADERS
*Youth, Innovators, Influencers*

### Unemployed Graduates (18-30)
- **Chioma (22, Economics):** *"Show me someone who earned and withdrew today. Then I will believe."*
- **Power:** Low institutional, High viral potential
- **Need:** Dignified work with verifiable history

### University Tech Communities
- University of Abuja CS, Cardano Nigeria, Web3 Ladies
- **Power:** Medium tech influence
- **Need:** Real Cardano dApp to contribute to

### Climate Activists & Content Creators
- **Power:** Medium reach, Low structural change
- **Need:** Real solutions to amplify

---

## AFFECTED GROUPS
*Those Living With the Problem*

### Residents of Formal Neighborhoods
- **Mrs. Adebayo (Gwarimpa):** *"I paid. They did not come. What can I do? Report to who?"*
- **Power:** Medium (can switch providers)
- **Need:** Service verification proof

### Residents of Informal Settlements
- Dutse, Lugbe extension - zero formal services
- **Power:** Very low political visibility
- **Need:** Any collection at all, health protection

### Market Traders
- Kubwa Market, Dutse Market - high daily waste volume
- **Power:** Medium collective bargaining
- **Need:** Consistent daily collection

### School Communities
- Primary schools near dumpsites
- **Power:** Low direct, High moral authority
- **Need:** Clean environments, safety

## Power Dynamics

**Who Has Power But Lacks Information:**  
Government (AEPB, Ministry) - Budgets and authority but operates blind

**Who Has Information But Lacks Power:**  
Informal Collectors - Know daily ground reality but trapped in shadow economy

**Who Has Money But Needs Proof:**  
Corporates & Funders - ESG budgets waiting for verified impact data

**Who Does Work But Gets No Credit:**  
Collectors, Health Workers - Daily labor with zero institutional recognition
