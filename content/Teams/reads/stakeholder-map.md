This map identifies the primary persons, groups, and organizations relevant to the success and adoption of the $READS initiative.

## Caretakers
*The Support System*

**Examples/Roles:** Parents, guardians, teachers

**Influence/Needs:**
- Need tools to support student learning
- Resources to provide guidance and encouragement
- Transparent progress tracking
- Affordable alternatives to expensive prep platforms

---

## Stakeholders
*The Institutions*

**Examples/Roles:** Exam boards (JAMB, WAEC), schools and universities, local government

**Influence:**
- Provide infrastructure, regulation, and official access
- Control exam registration processes
- Set educational standards

**Needs:**
- Must be incentivized to adopt $READS for official recognition and verification
- Transparent systems for tracking student progress
- Verifiable certification systems

---

## Emerging Leaders
*The Disrupters*

**Examples/Roles:** Youth leaders, campus ambassadors, social influencers

**Influence:**
- Promote adoption and drive peer engagement
- Provide ground-level feedback
- Act as bridge between students and platform

**Needs:**
- Simple, shareable tools
- Recognition for their advocacy
- Incentives for community building

---

## Affected Groups
*Those Living With the Problem*

**Examples/Roles:** Students, low-income families, underfunded schools

**Needs:**
- Affordable, accessible, motivating education tools
- Solutions that address infrastructure gaps (power/internet)
- Verifiable certificates that employers and institutions trust
- Financial support for exam registration

---

## Tech Partners
*The Infrastructure Builders*

**Examples/Roles:** Blockchain developers, app developers, Africa's Talking/Twilio for USSD

**Influence:**
- Build technical infrastructure
- Ensure secure, inclusive access
- Enable USSD and offline functionality

**Needs:**
- Clear APIs and robust documentation
- Defined integration requirements
- Cardano blockchain expertise
