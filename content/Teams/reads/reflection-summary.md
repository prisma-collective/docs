## What We Initially Believed

We initially thought the main issue was the lack of quality study materials and assumed that simply providing digital lessons and Computer-Based Test (CBT) practice alone would significantly improve student performance. 

We also operated under the assumption that most learners had stable internet access and that existing online certificates were generally trusted.

---

## What We Discovered (Key Realizations)

Through deeper reflection and interactions with our community, we realized the real challenges were far more fundamental:

**Motivation Gap:** Students often lack internal motivation systems for continuous study.

**Affordability:** Many cannot afford the paid subscriptions of leading study applications.

**Accessibility:** A significant number of learners rely on USSD or basic phones, not consistent broadband internet.

**Credibility:** Online certificates are often not verifiable, significantly reducing their professional and academic trust.

---

## Impact on Our Final Project Idea

These realizations fundamentally reshaped $READS into a more inclusive solution:

| Former Focus | New $READS Focus |
|--------------|------------------|
| Digital Content Only | Learn-to-Earn Model to boost motivation and reward effort |
| Stable Internet Required | USSD/Offline Access to ensure inclusivity and reach all learners |
| Standard Online Certificate | Blockchain-Verifiable Certificates to ensure trust and credibility |

---

## Core Insight

The final project now centers on providing an experience that is **affordable, engaging, rewarding, credible, and truly accessible** for all learners, regardless of their infrastructure constraints.

**$READS is not just another EdTech platform. It is a dignity engine that transforms study effort into economic value.**
