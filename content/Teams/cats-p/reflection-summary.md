# 💭 Team Reflection Summary — CATS_P

**The Road to Clarity**

---

## What Shifted in Our Perspective

Our initial perspective was a broad focus on the **economic inconvenience of vehicle breakdowns** in rural Nigeria. However, after engaging with stakeholders and observing the flow of traffic around Abuja's key transit junctions (like Zuba and the Airport Road interchange), our understanding shifted significantly **from a problem of inconvenience to one of vulnerability and trust**.

We started thinking we were solving a logistics problem—"How do we get mechanics to broken-down cars faster?"

What we discovered was actually a **trust problem**—"How do we make drivers confident enough to use the skilled mechanics who are already nearby?"

This reframing changed everything about our solution approach.

---

## What Surprised and Moved Us

What surprised and moved us the most was realizing that motorists were often willing to pay a hefty **"trust tax"**—enduring a 3-hour wait and paying three times the service cost—just to have their known mechanic drive out from the city center, rather than using a closer roadside mechanic.

**This was profound.**

Drivers would rather:
- Wait 3 hours in a potentially unsafe location
- Pay 3x the normal cost
- Lose half a day of productivity
- Risk missing critical appointments

...than trust a nearby, qualified mechanic they couldn't verify.

This illustrated that **the challenge isn't just a lack of mechanics, but a total lack of verification and digital visibility for the skilled mechanics who are already strategically positioned along the outskirts**.

The mechanics are there. The skills are there. The demand is there.

**What's missing is the trust layer.**

---

## What the Community is Asking For

The community, specifically the drivers and the local roadside mechanics, appears to be asking for a **neutral digital intermediary**.

### The Drivers Are Asking For:

- **Safety:** "Is this mechanic real and qualified?"
- **Transparency:** "What will this cost me?"
- **Assurance:** "Will they actually fix my car?"
- **Speed:** "How fast can they get here?"

### The Local Mechanics Are Asking For:

- **Access:** "How do I reach customers beyond my immediate roadside?"
- **Credibility:** "I am skilled; I just need a platform to prove it and receive jobs"
- **Fair income:** "Stop the middlemen who take most of the payment"
- **Recognition:** "My work is professional—let me show it"

---

## What This Means for Our Solution

Our solution, **Road_mech**, must prioritize building this **digital layer of trust** between these two essential groups.

The platform is not just about connecting supply and demand—it's about creating **verifiable proof of competence** that allows trust to exist between strangers in vulnerable situations.

**Key Design Principles:**

1. **Verification First:** Mechanic credentials, skills, and background checks must be rigorous and visible
2. **Transparency Always:** Pricing, location, ETA, and service history must be clear upfront
3. **Reputation Matters:** Rating and review systems must be prominent and trustworthy
4. **Speed Counts:** But never at the expense of safety and verification

---

## Core Realization

We thought we were building a mechanic-finder app.

We're actually building a **trust infrastructure** that transforms informal roadside labor into a verified, professional service network.

The mechanics don't need to change. The skills don't need to improve. What needs to change is the **visibility and verifiability of their competence**.
